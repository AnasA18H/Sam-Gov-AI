Statement of Work Stack and Rack Carts SECTION I: Background The Bureau of Engraving and Printings (BEP) mission is to develop and produce United States currency notes, trusted worldwide. BEPs vision is to be the world standard securities printer providing its customers and the public with superior products through excellence in manufacturing and innovation. As its primary function, the BEP prints billions of dollars, referred to as Federal Reserve notes, each year for delivery to the Federal Reserve System. As part of the printing and delivery process, Stack and Rack Carts (also referred to as Carts) are used for separating wet work during the drying process after Intaglio printing. These carts are specific to press size and include several accessories including metal rods and security hoods. SECTION II: Purpose The purpose of this requirement is to establish a single contract for Stack and Rack Carts to be used at the BEP. Contractor shall provide the BEP with finished carts for use in the internal production process. Upon completion of Offset and Intaglio print phase, carts will be used to hold sheets for no less than seventy-two (72) hours of drying time. Carts are then offloaded and reused. During dry times, each cart will be secured and strapped with a security hood and locking mechanism. Mechanism will be provided by the BEP. SECTION III: Technical Requirements The Contractor shall manufacture and deliver Carts that are compliant with the specified requirements in the Statement of Work (SOW), Technical Requirements Data Sheet (TRDS) and drawings. Finished equipment shall be delivered to the Ft. Worth, TX Currency Facility. The following outlines the specifications for the Carts: Stack and Rack Carts: Carts shall be manufactured in accordance with the specifications set forth in Attachment A, Drawing 55222AD REV A (50-subject Stack and Rack Cart). In the event any part does not work or is deemed damaged or found to be unacceptable the Contractor will replace the item at no cost to the Government. The government will evaluate contractors performance utilizing two methods: Random Sampling: The COR technical representative will facilitate or conduct random sampling

in accordance with the Acceptable Quality Limits outlined in the requirements to ensure equipment meets requirements.
